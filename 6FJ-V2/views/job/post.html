Looking for a job online can be either enjoyable or frustrating for executives. The good news is you get to decide. With the right knowledge, you can be ahead of the majority job seekers and get more quality interviews and offers.

Here are 6 practical ideas to help you succeed in your online job search:

1. Use only reputable and level-appropriate talent communities:

What use is posting your resume on a job board that caters to recent college graduates if you are looking for an executive position? Many job seekers believe that the more ‘exposure’ they have, the better. The reality is you are better off using executive sites such as 6FigureJobs to give you access to career opportunities you are qualified for.

2. Be active online if you want better results.

Some executives believe that merely having an online profile is enough to get loads of interviews. It usually isn’t, unless you are in a very specialized field. Don’t wait for employers to find you! It is far more effective to do periodic searches for new postings and apply without delay.

3. Get a high-power resume that doesn’t get lost in a pile.

Millions of resumes are e-mailed daily. How does your resume stack up? Here is the solution: develop a document that sends this messages: “I am positively different from other qualified applicants and you will likely be interested in what I have to offer”. It’s a common mistake to use a form-based resume and expect results.
